About

My name is Lars Falk. My colleagues and I have, over more than three decades, developed a programme and concept for locating and evaluating suitable land areas to establish wells for producing and securing drinking water from groundwater sources in Scandinavia.

Today, our water resources are a highly topical issue, and public awareness of the importance of having access to clean water cannot be taken for granted.

To supply 100,000 people with approximately 3 litres of drinking water per day requires access to an extraction of 300,000 litres per day — or 54,750,000 litres per year — from a secured well or source.

Over the years, we have continuously updated our development and action plan with strategic methods to secure groundwater sources for the target groups we were in dialogue with as far back as the 1990s. At that time, there was no need to develop private wells within a classified groundwater source.

Today, the global situation is entirely different. Our chosen target groups are the Armed Forces, Police and Rescue Services, MCF, Municipalities, and Institutions.

We have chosen to place a high level of confidentiality on our groundwater development and action plan. We present no details on our website — this is communicated verbally upon request, during a physical meeting.
